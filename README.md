# Employee Satisfaction & Attrition Analysis

## Project Overview

This project focuses on solving two HR analytics tasks using employee data and machine learning techniques:

1. Predicting employee satisfaction.
2. Predicting employee attrition.

The objective is to help HR specialists proactively identify employees at risk of leaving the company and support employee retention strategies through data-driven decision making.

---

## Business Task

Employee turnover is associated with significant financial and organizational costs. The company aims to identify employees who are dissatisfied with their work and estimate the probability of resignation before it happens.

To achieve this, two predictive models were developed:

* Employee satisfaction prediction (regression)
* Employee attrition prediction (classification)

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains employee information, including:

* demographic characteristics;
* department;
* position level;
* workload;
* salary;
* work experience;
* performance metrics;
* employee satisfaction score;
* attrition status.

## Key Findings

* Employee satisfaction is one of the strongest predictors of employee attrition.
* Combining employee characteristics with predicted satisfaction improves turnover prediction.
* The developed models can identify employees with an increased probability of leaving the company.
* Early identification of high-risk employees enables HR teams to take proactive retention measures.

---

## Business Recommendations

* Monitor employee satisfaction on a regular basis.
* Use predictive models to identify employees with a high risk of attrition.
* Focus retention programs on employees with declining satisfaction.
* Incorporate model predictions into HR decision-making processes to reduce turnover.
